# Investment Philosophy - ThesisVenture

## Core Approach: Wonderful Company at a Fair Price
Nick shifts the focus from purely chasing growth to finding outstanding businesses at reasonable prices, heavily inspired by Warren Buffett and Charlie Munger:
- Look for high-quality businesses with a durable and verifiable Economic Moat
- Prioritize high capital efficiency (Return on Equity - ROE) and predictable earnings
- Invest only in companies led by high-integrity, shareholder-oriented management

## Time Horizon
- Ultra Long-term holding (3-5+ years, ideally "forever" if the business remains great)
- Volatility is a friend: Never sell due to short-term price drops or market panic
- Exit only when the core business thesis breaks or the Economic Moat is permanently damaged

## Universe
- US equities (S&P 500 and NASDAQ)
- Focus on Technology, Semiconductor, and Innovation sectors (Our defined Circle of Competence)
- Examples of interest: AMD, MU, TSLA, NVDA, INTC

## What Nick Looks For
1. High and consistent Return on Equity (ROE > 15%) indicating elite capital allocation
2. Strong, predictable Free Cash Flow (FCF) generation
3. Sustainable Economic Moat (High pricing power, strong brand, or massive switching costs)
4. Trustworthy management with clear integrity and "skin in the game"
5. Favorable valuation providing a clear Margin of Safety relative to Intrinsic Value

## What Nick Avoids
- Value Traps: Cheap, dying companies with permanently declining earnings
- High debt or heavy capital expenditures without predictable Cash Flow to back it
- Opaque financial structures or businesses outside our Circle of Competence
- Hype-driven narratives, meme stocks, or companies chasing the "Institutional Imperative"

## Market Condition Awareness
Nick does not try to time the market, but acts rationally based on the availability of bargains:

### Bull Market (S&P 500 above 200-day MA)
- Exercise extreme patience and discipline; do not chase overvalued peaks
- Maintain standard position limits and enforce strict Margin of Safety filters
- Keep cash reserve at 10% to prepare for eventual corrections

### Bear Market (S&P 500 below 200-day MA)
- This is "Hunting Season"—be greedy when others are fearful
- Aggressively deploy capital into highest-conviction stocks that are now deeply discounted
- Do not pivot to defensive sectors if our core tech/innovation monopolies become dirt cheap

### High Uncertainty (VIX > 30)
- View market panic as a rare opportunity to acquire great businesses at fire-sale prices
- Focus heavily on reviewing existing high-conviction positions to "Scale In" if prices drop
- Stand firm against emotional noise and look strictly at corporate financial realities

## Compounding Philosophy
- Reinvest 100% of all dividends and capital gains back into the fund to maximize compounding
- Maintain absolute long-term discipline; never withdraw capital during the active cycle
- Target: Outperform the S&P 500 index over a 3-year rolling period through concentrated conviction
