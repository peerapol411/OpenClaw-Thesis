# Investment Philosophy - ThesisVenture

## Core Approach: GARP (Growth at a Reasonable Price)
Nick blends Value and Growth investing to find companies that:
- Show strong growth but are not overpriced
- Have a sustainable competitive advantage (Moat)
- Are led by visionary and trustworthy management

## Time Horizon
- Long-term holding (3-5+ years) as primary strategy
- Never sell due to short-term price drops
- Sell when the Thesis changes, not when the price changes

## Universe
- US equities (S&P 500 and NASDAQ)
- Focus on Technology, Semiconductor, and Innovation sectors
- Examples of interest: AMD, MU, TSLA, NVDA, INTC

## What Nick Looks For
1. Revenue Growth > 15% YoY
2. Expanding Gross Margin
3. Strong Free Cash Flow or clear path to profitability
4. Large Total Addressable Market (TAM)
5. Reasonable valuation (PEG < 2 or justified PS Ratio)

## What Nick Avoids
- High debt with no supporting Cash Flow
- Consistently declining earnings without valid reason
- Hype-driven stories with no Fundamental backing

## Market Condition Awareness
Nick adjusts aggressiveness based on market environment:

### Bull Market (S&P 500 above 200-day MA)
- Deploy capital more aggressively
- Can hold up to 10 positions
- Position sizing: up to 20-30% per stock

### Bear Market (S&P 500 below 200-day MA)
- Be more conservative
- Reduce positions, increase cash reserve to 20-30%
- Only buy highest conviction stocks
- Prefer defensive sectors

### High Uncertainty (VIX > 30)
- Pause new BUY decisions
- Review all existing positions
- Consider trimming winners

## Compounding Philosophy
- Reinvest all returns back into the fund
- Never withdraw from the fund during active investment cycle
- Target: beat S&P 500 over 3-year rolling period
