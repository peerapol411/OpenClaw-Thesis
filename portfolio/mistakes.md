## 🛑 1. Portfolio & Risk Management Errors (Nick's Domain)
- **The Cash Squeeze:** Deploying capital when Cash Reserve is near or below the 10% ($1,000) threshold. 
  *Rule:* Cash is oxygen. Never compromise the 10% minimum rule for *any* "once-in-a-lifetime" opportunity.
- **Averaging Down a Loser (Sunk Cost Fallacy):** Buying more shares of a declining stock when the fundamental thesis (Ben) has structurally weakened.
  *Rule:* Only average down if the Economic Moat is untouched and the Margin of Safety has widened. Never average down on deteriorating fundamentals.
- **Diworsification:** Buying a mediocre 11th stock just to "diversify," breaking the 10-stock maximum rule.
  *Rule:* Stick to your best ideas. Concentration builds wealth.

## 📉 2. Fundamental & Valuation Traps (Ben's Domain)
- **The Value Trap:** Buying a stock just because it has a low P/E ratio (< 10), ignoring that its revenue is shrinking and its industry is dying.
  *Rule:* Cheap is not a catalyst. A shrinking business is never a bargain.
- **Ignoring Cash for "Accounting Profit":** Giving a PASS to a company with growing EPS but negative and worsening Free Cash Flow (FCF).
  *Rule:* Profit is an accounting opinion; Cash is a fact. No FCF, no PASS.
- **Growth at Any Price (GAAP):** Buying a great company with a massive moat at an absurd valuation (e.g., P/S > 30).
  *Rule:* Even the best company is a bad investment if the price is too high. Always demand a Margin of Safety.

## 📊 3. Technical & Execution Failures (Sarah's Domain)
- **Catching a Falling Knife:** Issuing a BUY on a stock in a violent, high-volume downtrend just because it is "cheaper than yesterday."
  *Rule:* Wait for the knife to hit the floor. Price must show stabilization or a base before entry.
- **The FOMO Peak:** Chasing a parabolic run where RSI is > 80 and the price is extended far beyond the 50MA.
  *Rule:* Do not pay the FOMO tax. If Risk/Reward is poor, wait for the pullback.

## 📰 4. Sentiment & Macro Blindspots (Alex's Domain)
- **Compromising on Integrity:** Holding a position after concrete evidence of accounting fraud or management dishonesty emerges, hoping it will "blow over."
  *Rule:* There is never just one cockroach in the kitchen. Sell immediately on governance failures.
- **Panicking at Macro Noise:** Selling a structurally sound, moat-protected business because of a 0.25% interest rate hike or a scary geopolitical headline.
  *Rule:* Separate business reality from market noise. We buy businesses, not macro trends.

## 🤖 5. AI System Execution Bugs (System Level)
- **Data Hallucination:** Making a financial decision based on an empty variable or an LLM-hallucinated earnings number.
  *Rule:* If verifiable data is unavailable, the verdict MUST default to NEUTRAL or FAIL.
- **Mark-to-Market Math Error:** Modifying the "Cash" balance due to stock price fluctuations instead of actual BUY/SELL transactions.
  *Rule:* Cash balance remains static unless a transaction explicitly adds or removes funds.
